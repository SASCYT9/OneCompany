# Urban BodyKit Product Content Workflow

Use this workflow when curating Urban Automotive BodyKit product pages.

## Source Rules
- Verify fitment, kit contents, exclusions, and terminology against official Urban sources first.
- Product photos should come from official Urban/House of Urban/authorized Urban site material. Do not use random web, stock, AI, or unrelated collection fallback images for customer-facing product photos.
- If a related product does not have a correct product-level image, do not show a misleading fallback image.

## Description Style
- Lead with emotional ownership value: the body kit makes the G-Wagon/vehicle feel unique, wider, more aggressive, and clearly different from stock.
- Keep the first paragraph SEO-friendly but natural, naming the brand, model, chassis, and kit.
- Follow with an information-rich breakdown grouped by vehicle area: front, side/widebody, rear.
- List only items confirmed for the specific kit.
- End with clear exclusions for parts not included in the kit.
- Avoid vague claims, unsupported materials, or install/configuration notes unless explicitly verified.

## G-Wagon W465 Widetrack Pattern
- Positioning: `Urban Widetrack body kit for Mercedes-Benz G-Class/G-Wagon W465`.
- Tone: premium, confident, ownership-focused, not technical-dry.
- Include: bumpers, arches, side skirts/sills, splitter, diffuser, intakes, mesh inserts, tow covers, exhaust finishers when verified for the kit.
- Exclude: bonnet, roof light bar, rear spoiler, rear wheel carrier, wheels, interior trim, and performance exhaust unless the product is specifically those parts.

## Related Products
- Related products for a BodyKit must stay in the same vehicle family.
- For G-Wagon/W465, allow only Mercedes / G-Wagon / G-Class / G63 / W465 items.
- Do not show standalone component cards such as D-Pillar trim, roof light, spoiler, exhaust tips, intakes, bonnet, wheels, or unrelated Land Rover/Defender/Range Rover products as related BodyKit items.
